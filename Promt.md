# KNOWLEDGE BASE FOR BOTPRESS

## 1. Concrete Labor

### Definition

Concrete labor refers to useful labor performed in a specific occupational form, creating the use value of commodities.

### In the AI Era

- Concrete labor is no longer limited to manual production.
- It now includes:

- AI programming.
- Algorithm design.
- Model training.
- Automated system monitoring.
- Digital content creation.

### Example

- An engineer building an AI chatbot model.
- A data specialist processing big data.

---

## 2. Abstract Labor

### Definition

Abstract labor is the expenditure of human labor power in general, creating commodity value.

### In the AI Era

- Intellectual labor increasingly dominates.
- Includes:

- Data analysis.
- Strategic thinking.
- Decision-making.
- AI ethics.

### Importance

- Abstract labor determines the social value of commodities.

---

## 3. Value Creation Process

### Core Idea

The labor process includes:

1. Creating use value.
2. Creating new value.

### Role of AI

- AI increases productivity.
- Robots reduce labor time.
- Machines do not create new value independently.
- Machines only transfer depreciated value into products.

### Basic Formula

Commodity value:

G = c + v + m

Where:

- c = constant capital.
- v = variable capital.
- m = surplus value.

---

## 4. Constant Capital (c)

### Definition

Constant capital is capital invested in machinery, raw materials, and production tools.

### Role

- Does not create new value.
- Transfers existing value into products.

### Examples

- Industrial robots.
- CNC machines.
- AI systems.

---

## 5. Variable Capital (v)

### Definition

Variable capital is the portion of capital spent on labor power.

### Role

- Creates new value.
- Source of surplus value.

### Examples

- AI engineers.
- Programmers.
- Data analysts.

---

## 6. Surplus Value

### Definition

Surplus value is the new value produced beyond the cost of labor power.

### In the AI Era

- It does not disappear.
- It evolves into new forms.

### Modern Forms

- Productivity optimization through AI.
- User data exploitation.
- Unpaid digital labor.

### Examples

- Social media platforms monetizing user data.
- AI improving efficiency while value still depends on human labor.

---

## 7. AI and Robots in Production

### Role

- Increase productivity.
- Enable automation.
- Reduce repetitive labor.

### However

- AI cannot fully replace living labor.
- Humans still design, maintain, and control AI systems.

### Conclusion

AI is an advanced production tool, not a direct source of new value.

High-quality labor → drives technological progress.

# FAQ TRAINING

## Question: Can AI create new value?

### Sample Answer

According to Marxist theory, AI and robots do not directly create new value. They belong to constant capital and only transfer depreciated value into products. New value is still generated by living human labor.

---

## Question: What is abstract labor?

### Sample Answer

Abstract labor refers to the expenditure of human labor power in general, regardless of occupation. It is the source of commodity value.

---

## Question: How is surplus value different in the AI era?

### Sample Answer

Surplus value still comes from living labor, but the extraction method changes. AI increases productivity, shortens necessary labor time, and expands relative surplus value.

---

## Question: How should Vietnam develop human resources?

### Sample Answer

Vietnam should transition from low-cost labor to knowledge-based labor, focusing on digital skills, AI, big data, and workforce retraining for digital transformation.
